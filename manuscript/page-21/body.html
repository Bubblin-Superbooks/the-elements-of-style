Two-part sentences of which the second member is introduced by as (in the sense of because), for, or, nor, and while (in the sense of and at the same time likewise require a comma before the conjunction. )

If the second member is introduced by an adverb, a semicolon, not a comma, is required (see ). The connectives so and yet may be used either as adverbs or as conjunctions, accordingly as the second clause is felt to be co-ordinate or subordinate; consequently either mark of punctuation may be justified.
